What Is Antisemitism?

Antisemitism means prejudice against or hatred of Jews. In the United States, antisemitic incidents continue to rise yearly. Hatred of Jews can take many forms, including violent attacks. It also appears in daily life.

Supporting Israel And Fighting Antisemitism

Understanding and responding to antisemitism is necessary, but the ultimate goal must be to prevent it. Antisemitism impacts millions of Jews and people of all backgrounds in the U.S. and around the globe, tearing the fabric of societies wherever it spreads. ADL is the world’s leading expert on antisemitism and is on the front lines, shining a light on all forms of antisemitism and empowering individuals and communities with tools to address this age-old hate.

How Prevalent is Antisemitism Online?

Almost two-thirds of Jewish Americans (67%) have seen antisemitism online or on social media in the past year. Even more, one-in-eight Jewish Americans have reported being personally targeted by antisemitic remarks or posts online or through social media in the past year.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the role of bystanders in preventing antisemitic incidents?

Bystanders play a crucial role by speaking out against antisemitic incidents, reporting hate crimes, and promoting a culture of tolerance. Silence can inadvertently contribute to the perpetuation of discrimination.

How does antisemitism manifest in modern society?

Modern antisemitism can manifest in different ways, including hate speech, vandalism, online harassment, and discriminatory policies. It may also be expressed through conspiracy theories targeting Jewish individuals or communities.

How can interfaith dialogue contribute to reducing antisemitism?

Interfaith dialogue fosters understanding and cooperation between different religious communities, helping to break down stereotypes and promote tolerance. Building relationships between diverse groups can contribute to a more inclusive society.

What is the role of education in combating antisemitism?

Education plays a crucial role in combating antisemitism by promoting awareness, understanding, and tolerance. Teaching about the Holocaust and fostering respect for diversity can help counter stereotypes and prejudices.

What are the historical roots of antisemitism?

Antisemitism has deep historical roots, with instances dating back centuries. Factors include religious differences, economic scapegoating, and conspiracy theories. The Holocaust during World War II is a tragic example of the extreme consequences of antisemitism.
